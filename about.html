My career journey has taken me across disciplines, from media planning, to software engineering, and finally to user experience. While I got a taste of fusing my dual passion in psychology and technology in digital marketing, I knew I wanted to take it a step further and pursue a career in tech to create powerful tools and intuitive experiences for industries I admire.

To start, media planning reinforced the importance of research, from user demographics and behavior to understanding the specific business and industry. Starting with a thorough review of the client, we would analyze and build upon existing campaigns, incorporating internal and external feedback, A/B testing, and analytics.

Curating ad funnels, it was crucial to grasp the entire user journey in order to escort potential customers from awareness, to consideration, to conversion. These ads had to match and accentuate the brand’s style of copy and design to facilitate users transitioning seamlessly from ad to landing page; more than anything, the users' interaction with the ads needed to feel effortless, even enjoyable.

Switching to software engineering, the focus became building features that will achieve specific goals. From pseudocoding, to wireframing, to prototyping, the planning phase is critical for setting a solid foundation for any project; it has to ensure that the features are technically feasible, the design style matches the client or theme, and requires mapping the entire journey for users.

Through the development process, it’s essential to be aware of best practices, prioritize the mvp, and constantly clean code, reiterating over any elements that can be improved. To finish, usability testing allows developers to uncover any issues, gather feedback, and ensure they are able to deliver a fully functional web application.

The culmination of these experiences encapsulates the essential qualities of a UX researcher and designer, and I’m eager now to use these skills building the next generation of software that supports music, health, and productivity.
